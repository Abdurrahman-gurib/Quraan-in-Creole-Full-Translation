Quraan Translation in Creole

Bringing the sacred text of the Quraan closer to the Creole-speaking community.

About the Translation

The translation of the Quraan into Mauritian Creole serves to provide the Creole-speaking population with the opportunity to connect with the teachings and wisdom of the Quraan in their native language. Through this project, we aim to preserve the authenticity of the Quraan while making its message easily understandable.

The translation process has been carefully executed by experts, ensuring that each verse conveys the intended meaning in a manner that resonates with the cultural and linguistic characteristics of Creole.

Sample Translations

Surah Al-Fatiha (Chapter 1)

Original (Arabic): بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم

Creole Translation: Dan nom Bondie, bienfeteur, miséricordieux.

Surah Al-Baqarah (Chapter 2, Verse 2)

Original (Arabic): ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ

Creole Translation: Sa liv-la, pa ena okenn dout ladan, i enn gid pou bann ki krazrwa.

Surah Al-Ikhlas (Chapter 112, Verse 1)

Original (Arabic): قُلْ هُوَ ٱللَّهُ أَحَدٌ

Creole Translation: Dir: Bondie, i enn sel.

Why Translate the Quraan into Creole?

The Creole language is spoken by millions of people, yet many Creole speakers have limited access to the Quraan due to the language barrier. By providing a Creole translation, we ensure that more people can understand and engage with the Quraan’s teachings on spirituality, morality, and social guidance.

This translation project aims to:

  • Enhance Accessibility: Making the Quraan more accessible to those who speak Creole.
  • Promote Understanding: Help Creole-speaking individuals better understand the message of the Quraan.
  • Preserve the Message: Ensure that the spiritual teachings of the Quraan remain clear and relevant in Creole.
